Jun 2022 - Sept 2022

Process Mining and RPA Development - PwC

During my second placement at PwC I worked within a team where we used process mining and RPA (Robotic Process Automation) to automate varying financial tasks within the clients business.

Process mining is where you use event logs to create dashboards and process flows to identify inefficiencies within the project by talking to process owners and analyzing the data allowing you to see where the bottlenecks are and what the “happy path” may be. This will identify areas where automation can be used to improve efficiency and minimize deviation from the “happy path”.

While on this project we primarily used UiPath for the process mining and RPA development to identify and improve inefficacies within the P2P (Purchase to Pay), O2C (Order to Cash) and R2R (Record to Report) processes.

Aug 2019 - Sept 2019

Software Engineer (Work Experience Programme) - PSNI

I was part of the Sentinus IT Bursary Scheme which connected me to the PSNI to work on some projects during the end of the summer while I was doing my A-Levels.

One of these projects was a prototype facial recognition system, which was programmed in python. It would take 2 images as input and would identify landmarks on each of the faces of the people (or person) in the image and give a confidence score on how likely that the person from image 1 is the same as any persons in image 2. It also added bounding boxes around all faces in the images so the user can tell which person the algorithm thinks is the most likely to be a match to the person in image 1.

I also worked on helping the PSNI set up a secure cloud storage system called box and created a program that allows the user to interact with spreadsheets on box, the program allowed the user to upload single records or upload records as a batch file, delete multiple records and edit these records as well.

In my final week of the placement I wrote a report comparing some of the most popular hand writing recognition software’s that were available on the market at the time to see which would be the best for the PSNI to digitise some of the older hand written case notes.

During this placement and the creation of these systems I had to work with a group of people on the PSNI and contact people at box, this helped me develop team working skills and improved my communication abilities. This experience took place over four weeks.
