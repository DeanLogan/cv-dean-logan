June 2023 - Sept 2023

CCA (Cloud Cost Assurance) - PwC

This placement I was working on a team that was responsible for developing a section of the CCA (Cloud Cost Assurance) tool. This tool was designed to assist clients in effectively managing their cloud-related expenses. It achieved this by providing clients with an all-encompassing dashboard, which not only showcased their current cloud infrastructure costs but also projected potential savings achievable by transitioning to different architectures or even alternative cloud service providers. The tool also offered clients tailored recommendations on how to limit their cloud expenditures, prioritizing these recommendations based on their feasibility for the client.

The CCA tool is modular, consisting of various "Jobs" that can be executed to cater to the specific needs of clients. These jobs encompass a range of tasks, including cost analysis of particular cloud services from specific providers and conducting carbon footprint analyses for these services.

The team I was working on was responsible for developing the cost analysis job for AWS compute services. This involved analysis of the clients current compute services which was done by using the various APIs available from AWS Cloud Watch which allowed us to gain the clients usage data. This data was then processed and analysed to determine what resources the client needs from their compute serivce then estimate the prices of alternatives, then estimate associated migration costs based on how different the cheapest service is from the clients current service.

The development of this project was carried out using Java, with Gradle serving as the primary build tool. Emphasizing a Functional Programming approach, the project prioritized code efficiency and scalability, given the extensive datasets it dealt with. Additionally, the project adhered to a Test-Driven Development (TDD) methodology, promoting clearer code and an expedited development process.

Furthermore, I was entrusted with leadership responsibilities by the Director overseeing the CCA project. I had the opportunity to manage Year 1 and Year 2 Technology Degree Apprentices (TDA) and served as a mentor to them. During this period, I conducted workshops to elucidate the usage of Azure DevOps, including ticket management and branching strategies, as well as the principles of Test-Driven Development and JUnit testing. This experience allowed me to further enhance my leadership and mentoring skills while contributing to the professional development of my colleagues.


Sept 2022 - Dec 2022

Tech Central (PwC Asset Development) - PwC

I had the opportunity to contribute to a Google Workspace add-on project known as "Data Lab Importer." This valuable asset empowered users to seamlessly import data from an internal data source called "Data Lab" directly into a Google Sheet.

Within a small, agile-oriented team, we undertook the development of this project using Google Apps Script. Our approach involved retrieving data from Data Lab by executing API calls tailored to the user's data request. For the user interface, we leveraged Google's Card Service.

Upon accessing the Workspace add-on, users were presented with the option to choose their desired data set for import into their Google Sheet. Once a data set was selected, users had the flexibility to refine the results through a series of parameter-based filters or even remove specific columns before importing the table into their Google Sheet. This streamlined process allowed users to precisely determine the location within their Google Sheet where they wanted their imported data to reside.


June 2022 - Sept 2022

Process Mining and RPA Development - PwC

During this placement, I had the privilege of working as part of a dynamic team that harnessed the power of process mining and Robotic Process Automation (RPA) to streamline various financial tasks for our client.

Process mining involved the meticulous analysis of event logs to construct informative dashboards and visual process flows. These tools (UiPath, & Celonis) enabled us to collaborate closely with process owners, dissect the data, and pinpoint inefficiencies within the project's workflows. By doing so, we could identify bottlenecks and discern the optimal or "happy path" for each process. This insightful analysis served as the foundation for identifying areas where automation could be strategically applied, ultimately leading to enhanced efficiency and a reduction in deviations from the ideal process flow.

Throughout this project, our team predominantly utilized UiPath to execute process mining and develop RPA solutions. Our focus extended to optimizing processes within the Purchase to Pay (P2P), Order to Cash (O2C), and Record to Report (R2R) domains. Our goal was to not only identify inefficiencies but also to implement automated solutions that would drive greater efficiency and accuracy across these critical financial processes.

Aug 2019 - Sept 2019

Software Engineer (Work Experience Programme) - PSNI

I participated in the Sentinus IT Bursary Scheme, which provided me with an opportunity to collaborate with the PSNI (Police Service of Northern Ireland) on various projects during the late summer while I was pursuing my A-Levels.

One of the notable projects I was involved in was the development of a prototype facial recognition system using Python. This system took two images as input and performed facial landmark identification on the individuals depicted. It also generated a confidence score to determine the likelihood that the person in image 1 matched any individuals in image 2. Additionally, the system added bounding boxes around all the faces in the images, allowing users to easily identify the most likely match to the person in image 1.

I also contributed to the setup of a secure cloud storage system called "Box" for the PSNI. I created a program that facilitated user interaction with spreadsheets stored on Box. This program allowed users to upload individual records or batch files, delete multiple records, and edit existing records.

In the final week of my placement, I compiled a comprehensive report that compared various popular handwriting recognition software options available on the market at the time. This analysis aimed to assist the PSNI in their efforts to digitize older handwritten case notes effectively.

Throughout this four-week placement, I had the opportunity to collaborate closely with a diverse group of individuals within the PSNI and establish connections with personnel at Box. This experience significantly enhanced my teamwork and communication skills.
